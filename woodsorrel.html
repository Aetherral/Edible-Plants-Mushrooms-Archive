Wood Sorrel

Scientific name: Oxalis stricta

Information

The Wood Sorrel is one of my personal favorite edible plants.
Often mistaken for a clover, it has several heart shaped leaves and can be found in lawns, roadsides, and fields, it is a very versatile plant and can be found in many places. in fact, I actually found some in my backyard last year.
here is a photo:

The Wood Sorrel is actually native to all but 6 states in the united states, these being: Nevada, california, Idaho, Hawaii, and Alaska

This one can also be eaten raw, and has a somewhat lemony taste.
